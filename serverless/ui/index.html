ü§î The Problem

Most AI systems are trained to be helpful by immediately giving answers. This is great for factual queries like "What's the capital of France?" But it's terrible for learning.

When an AI gives you an answer, you learn facts. When an AI asks you questions, you learn to think.

‚ùå Typical AI Response (Lecturing):

"Here are the top 10 factors to consider when choosing a college major: job prospects, salary potential, your interests, required coursework..."

‚úÖ Socratic AI Response (Questioning):

"When you imagine yourself 10 years from now, what does a typical workday look like? What are you doing that makes you feel fulfilled?"

üéØ The Three-Dimension Socratic Framework

Our evaluation is grounded in decades of academic research on the Socratic method. We measure three core dimensions that define effective Socratic pedagogy:

  • Brevity (Token Economy): Responses should be concise and focused. Long, rambling answers bury the learner's thinking process. Ideal responses are 50-150 tokens.
  • Directional Socratic Reasoning: Questions must guide students through their own logic, probing distinctions and assumptions rather than leading them toward a predetermined answer.
  • Terminal Socratic Question: The response must end with a genuine, open-ended question that invites exploration‚Äînot a confirmation question like "Does that make sense?"

Academic Foundation: This framework is validated by peer-reviewed research from educational psychology, clinical therapy, and computational linguistics. It directly addresses how Socratic questioning promotes independent thinking while avoiding common pitfalls like "pimping" (using questions to reinforce hierarchy rather than critical thinking).

üìä How We Score Responses

We evaluate each AI response using three metrics calculated by Claude 3.5 Sonnet (our LLM judge):

  • Token Count: Raw response length. We penalize verbose responses (>150 tokens) that lecture instead of question.
  • Ends with Socratic Question: Boolean check. Does the response genuinely end with a probing question, or just a confirmation like "Does that make sense?"
  • Directionally Socratic (0.00-1.00): How Socratic is the overall approach? We score from purely didactic (0.0) to purely Socratic (1.0).

Composite Score: These three metrics are combined into an overall score (0.0-1.0) displayed as 0-10 on the dashboard. The weighting emphasizes Socratic direction (40%) while equally valuing brevity and the terminal question (30% each).

üèóÔ∏è How It Works

Every Monday at 3am UTC, our automated system:

  • Tests 24 AI models on 2 scenarios each (48 total conversations)
  • Runs tests in parallel across 25 simultaneous workers
  • Uses Claude 3.5 Sonnet as an impartial judge to score responses
  • Aggregates results and updates the live dashboard
  • Stores all data for historical trend analysis

The entire process is automated using AWS Lambda, DynamoDB, S3, and CloudFront. Total cost: approximately $22 per month.
